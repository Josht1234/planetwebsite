Sun
- The center of our solar system
- Is 99.86% of the mass of our solar system
- It travels at 220 km per second
- The temperature inside the sun can reach 15*107 celsius
Mercury
- The first planet from the sun
- Smallest planet in our solar system
- A year on Mercury is just 88 days long
- Only two spacecraft have visted Mercury
Venus
- The second planet from the sun
- A day on Venus lasts for 243 Earth days
- Second brightest object in the night sky
- Is the hottest planet in our solar system
- One of Earths sister planets
- Was once believed to be like earth
- Only one spacecraft has landed on it
- Has a out of control Green House Effect
Earth
- Only planet we know of that habors life as we know it
- The third planet from the sun
- Is the home planet of Humankind
- Has a powerful magnetic field
Mars
- The fourth planet from the sun
- Is the other sister of the Earth
- Is believed to once habor life
- Is one of the most explored planets in our solar system
- Has the tallest mountain in the solar system
- Has the largest dust storms in the solar system
- In the next 20-40 million years Mars will have a ring
Jupiter
- The fifth planet from the sun
- Biggest planet in our solar system
- Has the most moons of any planet
- Has the shortest days of all planets lasting 9 hours and 55 minutes
- The Great Red Spot is a massive storm 3 times the size of earth
- Has a thin ring system
- 8 spacecraft have visted Jupiter
Saturn
- The sixth planet from the sun
- The only planet with visible rings
- Takes 30 earth years to orbit the sun
- Only 4 spacecraft have visted Saturn
Uranus
- The seventh planet from the sun
- Takes 84 earth years to orbit the sun
- Hits the coldest temperatures of any planet
- Has two sets of rings that are very thin and dark colored
- Only 1 spacecraft has visted by Uranus
Neptune
- The eighth planet from the sun
- Spins very rapidly on its axis only taking 18 hours to make one rotation
- Smallest of the Ice Gaints
- Has a very active climate
- Has 14 moons
- Only 1 spacecraft has visted Neptune
